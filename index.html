Message Encryption

Background: Encryption is a way to scramble messages so only the intended recipient can read them. The Caesar cipher is one of the oldest encryption methods, where each letter is shifted by a fixed number of positions in the alphabet.

How it works:

  • To encrypt: shift each letter forward by the key number
  • To decrypt: shift each letter backward by the key number
  • If you go past Z, wrap around to A (and vice versa)
Examples with key = 3:
Encrypt "CAT" → "FDW" (C+3=F, A+3=D, T+3=W)
Encrypt "DOG" → "GRJ" (D+3=G, O+3=R, G+3=J)
Encrypt "XYZ" → "ABC" (X+3=A, Y+3=B, Z+3=C - wraps around!)

Decrypt "FDW" → "CAT" (F-3=C, D-3=A, W-3=T)

Problem: You intercepted an encrypted message: "FRGH"

If it was encrypted with a Caesar cipher using key = 3, what was the original message?

Stack Computer

Background: Stack Computers use a special notation called Reverse Polish Notation (RPN). Instead of writing "3 + 4", we write "3 4 +" (numbers first, then operation).

How to convert regular math to RPN:

  1. Write numbers in the order they appear
  2. Put operations after their numbers
  3. For complex expressions, work from innermost parentheses outward
Simple Examples:
3 + 4 becomes → 3 4 +
5 - 2 becomes → 5 2 -
6 × 7 becomes → 6 7 ×

With Parentheses:
(2 + 3) × 4 becomes → 2 3 + 4 ×
5 + (6 × 7) becomes → 5 6 7 × +

Problem: Convert this expression to RPN: 7 × (5 + 3) - 4

Robot Grid Navigation

Background: A robot moves on a coordinate grid. The grid uses (x, y) coordinates where:

  • x = horizontal position (increases going right)
  • y = vertical position (increases going up)
  • The robot starts at position (0, 0)
Movement Commands:
Move RIGHT 2: adds 2 to x-coordinate
Move LEFT 3: subtracts 3 from x-coordinate
Move UP 1: adds 1 to y-coordinate
Move DOWN 4: subtracts 4 from y-coordinate

Example:
Start: (0, 0)
Move RIGHT 3 → (3, 0)
Move UP 2 → (3, 2)
Move LEFT 1 → (2, 2)
Final position: (2, 2)

Problem: The robot starts at (0, 0) and executes:

REPEAT 2 TIMES:
    Move RIGHT 3
    Move UP 2
    Move LEFT 1
END REPEAT

What is the robot's final position?

Photo Sharing Network

Background: In the BeaverGram social network, when you post a photo, it's seen by:

  • You (the poster)
  • All your direct friends
  • Your friends can show it to their other friends by "sharing"

The Network:

        Amy          Ben
         |            |
         |            |
        Carl -------- Dana -------- Emma -------- Finn

Lines show friendships

Friendships:

  • Amy is friends with: Carl
  • Ben is friends with: Dana
  • Carl is friends with: Amy, Dana
  • Dana is friends with: Carl, Ben, Emma
  • Emma is friends with: Dana, Finn
  • Finn is friends with: Emma

Problem: If each person posts one photo and all their friends share it exactly once with their other friends, whose photo will be seen by the most people total?

5
Card Sorting Station

Background: At a sorting station, workers stand in a line. Each worker:

  • Looks at cards passing by from left to right
  • If two adjacent cards are in wrong order, swaps them
  • Otherwise, lets them pass unchanged
  • Cards go through all workers once
Example with cards [3, 1, 2]:
Worker 1 sees: 3, 1 → swaps to 1, 3
Worker 1 sees: 3, 2 → swaps to 2, 3
Result after Worker 1: [1, 2, 3] ✓ Sorted!

One worker was enough for this example

Problem: You have 5 cards in complete reverse order: [5, 4, 3, 2, 1]

What is the minimum number of workers needed to guarantee the cards end up fully sorted as [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]?
